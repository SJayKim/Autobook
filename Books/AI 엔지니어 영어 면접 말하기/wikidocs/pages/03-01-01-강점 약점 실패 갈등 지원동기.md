# 11. Behavioral & Common Questions — 공통 질문 정답

> 프로젝트 외의 "사람" 질문들. 답의 재료는 모두 당신의 실제 경험에서 가져왔습니다.
> 여기서는 **STAR** 구조를 씁니다 (프로젝트 설명의 5-Beat와 다름).

---

## 0. STAR 구조 (행동 질문 전용)

"~한 적 있나요?(Tell me about a time ...)"류 질문은 이 4단계로:

| STAR | 한국어 | 시작 표현 |
|------|--------|-----------|
| **S**ituation | 상황 (배경) | "At my company, we had a situation where ..." |
| **T**ask | 내 과제 | "My job was to ..." |
| **A**ction | 내가 한 행동 | "So what I did was ..." |
| **R**esult | 결과 (숫자면 최고) | "As a result, ..." |

> 핵심: **"우리"가 아니라 "내가"** 한 일을 말하세요. 면접관은 당신의 기여를 봅니다.

---

## 1. What is your greatest strength? (강점)

**생각 메모:** 강점 = "파이프라인 전체를 본다" + 증거 프로젝트 하나.

> I'd say my biggest strength is seeing a problem as a **whole pipeline**, not just a model. A lot of engineers optimize the model in isolation, but real service quality comes from preprocessing, retrieval, serving, and evaluation working together. In one RAG project, instead of only tuning the search, I cleaned up the data first — I removed about **12 percent** noisy chunks out of 150,000 — and that alone lifted retrieval quality. That habit of zooming out to the whole system is what I bring.

> **핵심 표현:** "seeing a problem as a whole pipeline" / "in isolation"(고립적으로) / "zoom out to the whole system"(전체로 시야를 넓히다)

---

## 2. What is your greatest weakness? (약점)

**생각 메모:** 진짜 약점 + 개선 노력. **치명적이지 않고 실제적인** 것. 당신의 진짜 약점은 "넓이는 충분한데 클라우드 매니지드 환경(AWS/K8s) 깊이가 상대적으로 얕다" — 그리고 지금 학습 중. 솔직하고 안전합니다.

> Honestly, my breadth has sometimes run ahead of my depth in one specific area: **managed cloud**. I've done a lot with Docker and Docker Compose and built multi-container GPU serving myself, but most of that was self-hosted, not on a managed platform like AWS or Kubernetes. I noticed that gap, so I'm actively closing it — I've been doing hands-on work with AWS Bedrock and studying managed LLM services, so I can carry the same container setups into a cloud environment. It's a real gap, but it's one I'm already working on.

> **핵심 표현:** "my breadth ran ahead of my depth"(넓이가 깊이를 앞섰다) / "I noticed that gap, so I'm actively closing it"(그 갭을 인지하고 메우는 중) / "hands-on"(직접 해보는)
> **전달 팁:** 약점은 담담하게 인정 → 곧바로 "그래서 이렇게 하고 있다"로. 변명하지 마세요.

## 3. Tell me about a difficult technical problem you solved. (어려웠던 문제 — STAR)

**생각 메모:** 평가 파이프라인(비용)이나 스미싱(단축 URL)을 STAR로. 아래는 평가 파이프라인.

> **(Situation)** We had a summarization model already running in production, and we needed to evaluate about **600,000** of its outputs in a short window. **(Task)** My job was to design the whole evaluation pipeline — and the hard constraint was cost: calling an LLM judge for every quality dimension on 600,000 items would have been impossibly expensive. **(Action)** So I split the metrics into two groups — deterministic ones I compute without any LLM, and a single quality score that needs just one LLM call. For that one call, I bundled all five quality criteria into a single judge prompt instead of five separate ones. **(Result)** That cut the inference cost to about a **fifth** while keeping quality, and the whole thing ran unattended on cron. So whenever the production model changes, we catch any regression on the next cycle.

> **핵심 표현:** "the hard constraint was cost"(핵심 제약은 비용이었다) / "bundled five criteria into one"(다섯을 하나로 묶었다) / "catch a regression"(품질 저하를 잡다)
> *(다른 버전이 필요하면 `07_Project_05` 정답지를 그대로 쓰면 됩니다.)*

## 4. Tell me about a time you failed or made a mistake. (실패·실수)

**생각 메모:** 진짜 실수 + 교훈. vLLM 프로젝트에서 요약·분류·태깅을 한 로직에 묶었다가 디버깅이 힘들어 분리한 경험 — 실제이고, 성장 서사가 깔끔.

> Early in the vLLM serving project, I built summarization, classification, and tagging as **one big combined logic**. It worked at first, but as the rules grew, it became really hard to debug — when something broke, I couldn't tell which part caused it. That was my mistake: I optimized for "fewer moving parts" instead of "easy to reason about." So I **split it into separate agents** connected with LangGraph, and after that the summary-length convergence rate went up to about **92 percent** and debugging got much simpler. The lesson I took away was to **decompose complex work into small, observable units** from the start — and I've applied that to every project since, including the Reflexion agent.

> **핵심 표현:** "one big combined logic"(하나로 뭉친 로직) / "hard to debug"(디버깅이 힘든) / "decompose into small units"(작은 단위로 분해) / "the lesson I took away"(내가 얻은 교훈)
> **전달 팁:** 실패담은 **솔직하게 인정 → 구체적 교훈 → 이후 적용**의 3단. 작은 실수, 큰 교훈.

## 5. Tell me about a disagreement or conflict on a team. (갈등·협업)

**생각 메모:** Visit Busan — 기획자가 원하는 일정 로직 vs AI 추천 품질 사이 트레이드오프. MVP 범위 협의 + 2주 데모로 해결. 갈등을 "협업으로 푼" 이야기.

> On the Busan travel chatbot, there was real tension between what the **planner** wanted and what was technically sound. The planner wanted very detailed, fixed itinerary logic, but that conflicted with keeping the AI recommendations flexible and high-quality. Instead of arguing in the abstract, I proposed we **agree on an MVP scope** and run a **demo every two weeks**. That way, the planner could see real output early and give feedback, and I could show the tradeoffs concretely instead of debating them on paper. We converged quickly, and the chatbot ended up **live in production** on the official site. What I learned is that most disagreements dissolve once people are looking at the same working demo.

> **핵심 표현:** "real tension between A and B"(A와 B 사이 실질적 긴장) / "instead of arguing in the abstract"(추상적으로 다투는 대신) / "agree on an MVP scope"(MVP 범위에 합의) / "most disagreements dissolve once ..."(대부분의 갈등은 ~하면 사라진다)

## 6. Tell me about a time you took ownership / led something. (주도성·리더십)

**생각 메모:** MarketScope 1인 풀스택(기획~인프라~배포) 또는 Visit Busan AI 리드. 아래는 MarketScope.

> The clearest example is **MarketScope**, an AI commercial-district analysis SaaS I built completely solo — planning, design, frontend, backend, the AI agent, and the infrastructure. Nobody assigned it to me; I saw that small-business owners had to dig through several statistics sites by hand, so I built a service where one natural-language question gives you an AI analysis on a map. I took it all the way to a **live production release**, with **66 out of 66** end-to-end tests passing and real data for **1,650** districts in Seoul. Doing every layer myself taught me where the real handoffs and failure points are across a full stack.

> **핵심 표현:** "completely solo"(완전히 혼자) / "nobody assigned it to me"(아무도 시키지 않았다 — 주도성 신호) / "took it all the way to a live release"(실제 배포까지 끌고 갔다)

## 7. Why should we hire you? / Why are you a good fit? (왜 당신인가)

**생각 메모:** 차별점 한 줄로 못 박기. 평가 + 풀 파이프라인 + 배포까지.

> Three things. First, I cover the **whole pipeline** — collection, search, serving, generation, and evaluation — so I can see where problems really come from. Second, I have a skill that's still rare: I build the systems that **verify LLM quality in production at scale**, not just the models themselves. And third, I actually **ship** — my side project is live, and the Busan chatbot I led is still in production. So I bring breadth, a rare evaluation skill, and a track record of finishing.

> **핵심 표현:** "a skill that's still rare"(아직 드문 역량) / "I actually ship"(실제로 출시한다) / "a track record of finishing"(끝까지 해내는 이력)

## 8. Where do you see yourself in a few years? (몇 년 후)

> I want to become the person a team trusts to take an AI system **from idea to reliable production** — especially on the evaluation and operations side, which is where I think the field is heading. Models are getting easier to build; knowing they're actually good, safely and cheaply, is the hard part. That's the depth I want to own.

> **핵심 표현:** "from idea to reliable production"(아이디어에서 신뢰할 수 있는 운영까지) / "where the field is heading"(이 분야가 향하는 곳)

## 9. Why this company / this role? (지원 동기 — 템플릿)

> 회사마다 달라야 합니다. 아래 **빈칸 템플릿**에 면접 전 조사 내용을 채우세요.

> I'm drawn to this role for two reasons. First, **[회사의 제품/문제 — 예: you're running LLMs in production at real scale]**, and that's exactly the problem I've spent the last year on — evaluating and operating production LLMs. Second, **[팀/기술 방향 — 예: your focus on X]** lines up with where I want to grow. I'm not looking for just any AI role; I'm looking for one where **[내 강점]** actually matters, and this looks like that.

> **조사 체크리스트 (면접 전):** ① 회사 제품/주력 도메인 ② 최근 출시·블로그·채용공고 키워드 ③ 그 키워드와 내 프로젝트의 접점 1개.
> **핵심 표현:** "I'm drawn to this role because ..."(이 역할에 끌리는 이유는) / "lines up with where I want to grow"(내 성장 방향과 맞는다)

## 10. Do you have any questions for us? (역질문 — 절대 "없다" 금지)

> 좋은 역질문은 "나는 이 일을 진지하게 생각한다"는 신호입니다. 3개 준비:

| 질문 (English) | 무엇을 보여주나 |
|----------------|-----------------|
| "How do you currently evaluate whether your models are working well in production?" | 내 차별점(평가) 영역으로 대화를 끈다 |
| "What does the path from prototype to production look like on your team?" | 배포·운영 지향임을 드러낸다 |
| "What would success in this role look like in the first six months?" | 진지함 + 기대치 정렬 |

> **전달 팁:** 면접 중 들은 내용에 즉석 질문을 더하면 최고입니다: "You mentioned ___ earlier — could you tell me more about how that works?"
